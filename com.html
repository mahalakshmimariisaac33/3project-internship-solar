Astrotes: Comets

Comets are cosmic snowballs of frozen gases, rock, and dust that orbit the Sun and light up our skies with spectacular tails.

Comets originate in the outer parts of the solar system, mostly in the Kuiper Belt and the distant Oort Cloud. When they approach the Sun, they heat up and release gases, forming a glowing coma and a bright tail that can stretch millions of kilometers.
